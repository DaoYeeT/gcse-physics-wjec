# 1-3-1 Heat transfer, density and energy efficiency

**(Higher only)** Mechanism of conduction (mobile electrons in metals) and explanation of convection in terms of molecular behaviour are both higher-only statements.

**Key facts**
- Thermal energy is transferred by **conduction** (vibrations and, in metals, electron movement), **convection** (bulk fluid movement driven by density differences) and **radiation** (infrared EM waves; no medium needed).
- **Metals conduct best** because of mobile, delocalised electrons that transport kinetic energy quickly through the lattice. **(Higher only)**
- In convection, warmer fluid is less dense so it rises; cooler fluid sinks, creating convection currents.
- Domestic energy-saving measures include loft insulation, double glazing, cavity-wall insulation and draught excluders.
- Payback time $=$ installation cost $\div$ annual saving.
- Density $\rho = m/V$.

**Key equations**
- $\rho = m/V$
- $\text{Payback time} = \frac{\text{installation cost}}{\text{annual saving}}$

**Common misconceptions**
- "Insulators keep cold out" — insulators slow all heat flow; in winter they slow heat loss outwards.
- "Radiation needs a medium" — it does not.
- "Hot air rises because heat is a substance" — hot air rises because it is less dense.

**Worked example**
A house loses 4500 kWh of heat per year. Loft insulation costing £350 reduces this by 30%. Electricity costs 15 p/kWh. Find the payback time.

Annual saving $= 0.30 \times 4500 = 1350$ kWh $= 1350 \times £0.15 = £202.50$.

Payback $= 350 / 202.5 \approx 1.7$ years.
