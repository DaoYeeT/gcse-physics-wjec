# 2-3-1 Work, KE, GPE and Hooke's law

**(Higher only)** Quantitative KE, GPE and the area-under-the-$F$-$x$-graph relation $W = \tfrac{1}{2} F x$ are higher-only.

**Key facts**
- Work done is the energy transferred when a force moves through a distance: $W = Fd$ (with $d$ in the direction of $F$).
- An object can carry KE (motion), GPE (height), elastic PE (deformation) and thermal energy.
- **Hooke's law** $F = kx$ holds for an ideal spring within its **limit of proportionality**. Beyond that limit, the spring deforms permanently.
- The area under a force-extension graph equals the work done in stretching, which is the elastic PE stored. For a linear spring $W = \tfrac{1}{2} F x = \tfrac{1}{2} k x^2$.
- Car safety features (air bags, crumple zones, seat belts) increase the time of a collision so that $F = \Delta p / t$ falls and the force on the passenger is reduced.

**Key equations**
- $W = Fd$
- $KE = \tfrac{1}{2} m v^2$ **(Higher only)**
- $PE = mgh$ **(Higher only)**
- $F = kx$
- $W = \tfrac{1}{2} F x$ (linear spring, **Higher only**)

**Common misconceptions**
- "GPE depends on absolute height" — it depends on change in height relative to a chosen reference.
- "Carrying a box across a flat floor does work on the box" — the force is perpendicular to the motion, so no work is done on the box in the direction of motion.
- "Energy is lost" — energy is conserved; it is transferred (often to thermal energy in the surroundings).

**Worked example**
A 0.06 kg tennis ball falls 1.8 m. Ignoring air resistance, find the speed on impact.

$\Delta GPE = mgh = 0.06 \times 10 \times 1.8 = 1.08$ J, all becoming KE.

$$\tfrac{1}{2} m v^2 = 1.08 \Rightarrow v^2 = \frac{2.16}{0.06} = 36 \Rightarrow v = 6\ \text{m/s}$$
